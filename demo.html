What Is Sentiment Vision?

Sentiment Vision is a data intelligence tool that automatically collects news articles about our clients from dozens of media sources, extracts the content, and scores each article's sentiment. The result: a real-time picture of how the media landscape views each client, their industry, and their competitors.

The Core Idea

Not all media coverage is created equal. A mention in Reuters carries more weight than a mention on a vendor blog. Sentiment Vision applies a tiered weighting system to ensure that high-authority sources have proportionally more influence on the overall sentiment score. This gives clients an accurate, signal-over-noise view of their media presence.

Understanding the Three Gauges

Company Sentiment — Only articles that directly mention "Cologix" by name. This is the clearest signal of how the media views the brand specifically.

Media Tier Weighting: Why It Matters

This is the core differentiator. Not all media mentions are equal. A story in the New York Times has a different reach and credibility than a vendor marketing blog. Our weighting system ensures sentiment scores reflect this reality.

The Four Tiers

Tier Category Weight Example Sources Rationale
T1 National / International Media 3.0x Reuters, AP News, NPR, BBC, NYT Highest authority and reach. A mention here moves markets and shapes public perception. These sources have the largest readership and strongest editorial standards.
T2 Business / Tech Press 2.0x TechCrunch, CNBC, Bloomberg, Ars Technica, The Verge High authority within business and tech audiences. Decision-makers, investors, and industry leaders read these. Strong editorial standards, significant reach in B2B.
T3 Industry Trade Publications 1.0x Data Center Dynamics, Data Center Knowledge, TechTarget Baseline weight. These are the specialized publications read by industry professionals. Highly relevant but narrower audience. This is the default tier for client-specific sources.
T4 Vendor / Marketing Content 0.5x Company blogs, sponsored content, press releases Half weight. This content is often promotional or self-published. It's worth tracking for completeness, but should not drive the overall sentiment score.

How the Weighted Score Is Calculated

The formula: Weighted Sentiment = Sum(score x weight) / Sum(weight)

Example with 3 articles:
• Reuters article (T1, weight 3.0) with sentiment +0.4 → contributes 1.2
• TechCrunch article (T2, weight 2.0) with sentiment -0.1 → contributes -0.2
• DCD article (T3, weight 1.0) with sentiment +0.3 → contributes 0.3

Weighted avg = (1.2 + -0.2 + 0.3) / (3.0 + 2.0 + 1.0) = 1.3 / 6.0 = 0.217 (Positive)

Without weighting, the simple average would be (0.4 + -0.1 + 0.3) / 3 = 0.2. The weighted version better reflects reality: the positive Reuters coverage should carry more influence than the slightly negative TechCrunch piece.

Why These Specific Weights?

T1 at 3x — National media shapes public narrative. An AP wire story gets picked up by hundreds of local outlets. One Reuters article may reach more people than 50 trade articles combined.

T2 at 2x — Business press reaches decision-makers, investors, and partners. Coverage here directly impacts how the business community perceives a client.

T3 at 1x (baseline) — Industry trades are the core audience who already knows the client. Important for monitoring but represents a narrower, already-engaged audience.

T4 at 0.5x — Vendor content is inherently biased toward the publisher. Including it at half-weight prevents promotional content from inflating sentiment while still tracking it.

These weights are configurable in settings.yaml and can be adjusted per client need. The system is designed to start a conversation about what matters most.

Why This Matters for Cologix

Without weighting, the dashboard would say "everything is fine, you're positive" — burying the fact that the New York Times just published a negative story that millions of people will see. The C-suite, investors, and partners are far more likely to have read the NYT than Data Center Knowledge.

With weighting, the score drops to Neutral, which is the honest signal: yes, industry coverage is good, but there's a high-visibility concern that needs attention. That's the kind of nuance that drives better decisions.

And critically, the 15 industry articles still win — the score is positive-leaning, not negative. The weighting doesn't let one article override everything. It just ensures that article's outsized real-world reach is reflected proportionally in the score.

How Articles Get Matched to Cologix

For global sources (T1/T2): the system fetches all articles from the feed, then checks if the article's title or body contains any of these keywords (case-insensitive): "Cologix", "Data Centers", "Colocation", "Digital Edge", or "Digital Real Estate". Any match stores the article under Cologix.

For client-specific sources (T3): all articles from Cologix's dedicated feeds are stored directly — they're already topically relevant by source selection.

The system runs automatically via cron. Here's the pipeline from source to sentiment score.

1. Article Collection
The Python orchestrator pulls articles from all configured RSS feeds and search URLs. Global sources (Reuters, AP, etc.) are fetched once. Per-client sources (DCD, Data Center Knowledge) are fetched per client. Rate-limited to 1 request every 2 seconds per domain.
2. Content Extraction
For each article URL, the system visits the page and extracts clean article text using trafilatura (primary) with readability-lxml as fallback. Strips ads, navigation, sidebars. Extracts: title, author, date, full text, word count.
3. Client Keyword Matching
Global-source articles are checked against each client's keywords (company name, industries, competitors). Matching articles are stored with the appropriate client and media tier. Deduplication by URL prevents double-counting.
4. Sentiment Analysis
Uses VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner). Long articles are split into sentences; each scored individually. Near-zero sentences are filtered out to prevent neutral dilution. Final score: mean of significant sentence scores (-1.0 to +1.0).
5. Weighted Dashboard
The web dashboard queries all stored articles, applies media tier weights at query time, and renders the gauges, bars, and scores. Tier toggles allow real-time filtering. All data is queryable by client, sentiment, and media tier.
6. Client Management
New clients can be added through the web UI. Just enter a name, industries, competitors, and source feeds. The system auto-generates Google News search URLs for comprehensive coverage. Config stored as YAML for easy review.
